Effect of GBP on UK economy after Brexit

One effect we observed after Brexit is a sudden decline in value of the GBP.

We look at how the currency decline is the key indicator, how it has affected the economy and how it effects public sentiment. In this presentation the impact on economy is focused on the trading activities which consist of import and export, and further drill down to the services sector for highlights on causes of impact.

Another economy impact is the substantial change in stock market, where FTSE100 is chosen as the main indicator and how it contradicts the speculations. We then show on how the Brexit phenomena has effected the general public and decision makers that has a close relationship with the change in economy.

Finally we discovered that not all economic areas are impacted by Brexit, where the retailing volume stayed stable throughout before and after the vote.

Currency

The pound has fallen heavily against the EUR and USD after the Brexit vote. EUR has fallen to 3 year low post the results. Post Brexit the GBP to EUR has fallen in access of 7%.
Sources: Pound Sterling vs EUR, Pound Sterlingvs USD

Imports

We can clearly see the sudden rise in the cost of Import after the Brexit Referendum results. This is mainly due to the fall of EUR against the Pound which leads to higer import costs.
Source: UK Trade - Import

Exports

Even with the fall of GBP vs EUR the Export trade has been on an upward trend and continues to maintain that trend even though after the Brexit referendum.
Source: UK Trade - Export

Change of UK trade in goods

Between June 2016 and July 2016, the sum of exports in main commodity value increased by £541 million. This was attributed to an increase in oil’s exports of £361 million. while the sum of imports decreased by £-285 million. This was attributed to a decrease in cars import of £-251 million and oil and capital goods of £-100 million each.
Source: Office for National Statistics - UK Trade

How do consumers perceive the financial and economic situation ?

People have been perceiving the general economic situation is getting worse, this is further enhanced with Brexit. The GBP and economic perception follows a similar pattern, in a simple word it could be the currency is the main factor the general public considers how the economy is doing. The perception on financial situation is generally well most of the time, but is substantially also effected by Brexit phenomena.
